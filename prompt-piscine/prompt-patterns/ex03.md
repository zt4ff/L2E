# Prompt Transformation with Patterns

## Weak Prompt

`Write about climate change.`

## Output (Weak Prompt)

Climate change is a global issue caused by rising temperatures and greenhouse gases. It affects the environment and people around the world. Many countries are trying to address it.

## Instructional Pattern

`Write a 200-word explanation of climate change for high school learners.`

## Output

Climate change refers to long-term changes in Earth’s temperature and weather patterns. It is mainly caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. These activities release greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide, into the atmosphere. Greenhouse gases trap heat from the sun, which causes the planet to warm.

This warming leads to melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather events such as heatwaves and heavy rainfall. Climate change also affects agriculture, water supply, and wildlife. Some species struggle to survive as their habitats change.

To reduce climate change, governments and individuals can lower emissions by using renewable energy sources like solar and wind power. Saving energy, planting trees, and reducing waste also help. Understanding climate change is important because it affects the future of our planet and the lives of young people today.

## Comparative Pattern

`Compare the effects of climate change on coastal vs inland regions in 5 bullet points.`

## Output

- Coastal regions face rising sea levels and increased flooding.

- Inland regions experience heatwaves and drought more intensely.

- Coastal economies suffer from storm surges and fisheries disruption.

- Inland agriculture is threatened by changing rainfall patterns.

- Infrastructure damage differs, with coastal erosion vs inland water scarcity.

## Reflection

The weak prompt produces a generic response.
The instructional pattern adds audience and length constraints.
The comparative pattern introduces structure and focus, resulting in clearer and more actionable insights.
