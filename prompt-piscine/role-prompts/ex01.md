### What is climate change?

**AI's response:**:
Climate change is the long term change in Earth’s average temperature and weather patterns.

It is mainly caused by greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane. These gases come from burning fuel, cutting down forests, and industrial activities.

As a result, the planet warms. This leads to stronger heat waves, floods, droughts, rising sea levels, and more extreme storms.

In short, climate change is the warming of the Earth and the wide impact it has on weather, oceans, and living things.

#### Scientist role:

**Prompt**: You are a scientist who has decades of experience with investigating the physical, chemical and biological processess altering Earth's climate. What is climate change?

**AI's response:**:
Climate change is a long term shift in Earth’s climate system driven by changes in the planet’s energy balance.

At its core, it is about physics. More greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide trap outgoing infrared radiation. This creates a radiative imbalance, so the Earth retains more heat than it emits. The system then warms until a new equilibrium is reached.

Chemically, the problem is tied to how we alter atmospheric composition. Burning fossil fuels, cement production, and deforestation increase carbon dioxide. Agriculture and energy systems add methane and nitrous oxide. These gases persist and interact with atmospheric chemistry, clouds, and aerosols.

Biologically, ecosystems both influence and respond to climate. Forests and oceans absorb large amounts of carbon, but warming reduces their efficiency. Species shift ranges, coral reefs bleach, and food webs reorganize.

Climate change is not just warmer air. It includes ocean warming, ice sheet loss, sea level rise, altered rainfall patterns, and more frequent extremes. It is a system wide transformation of the coupled atmosphere, ocean, land, and biosphere driven largely by human activity.

#### Journalist role

**Prompt**: You are a journalist whose role is to keep people informed about what's happening in the world. What is climate change?

**AI's Response:**

Climate change is the ongoing shift in global weather patterns, mainly driven by human activity.

Over the past century, countries have burned large amounts of coal, oil, and gas. This releases greenhouse gases that trap heat in the atmosphere. As a result, the planet is warming.

The effects are visible worldwide. Heat waves are becoming more intense. Floods and droughts are more frequent. Glaciers are melting and sea levels are rising, threatening coastal cities.

Scientists agree that human activity is the main cause. Governments, businesses, and communities are now debating how fast and how far to cut emissions to limit the damage.

#### Critic role

**Prompt:** You are a critic who acts as an expert evaluator, interpreter and cultural mediator analyzing scientific work to foster deeper understanding. What is climate change?

**AI's Response**:

Climate change is more than a rise in temperature. It is a profound shift in the way Earth’s climate system functions, and in how humans understand their place within it.

Scientifically, it refers to long term alterations in temperature, precipitation, ocean circulation, ice cover, and atmospheric chemistry. These changes stem largely from the accumulation of greenhouse gases produced by industrial society. The physics is clear. When we thicken the atmospheric blanket, we retain more heat.

But as a critic, I see climate change as a test of interpretation. It challenges how we translate data into meaning. A graph of rising carbon dioxide is also a story about energy systems, economic growth, and political choices. Melting ice is both a geophysical signal and a cultural symbol of fragility.

Climate change is therefore not only an environmental phenomenon. It is a civilizational moment. It forces us to reconcile scientific knowledge with values, power structures, and responsibility. It reveals how tightly woven the natural world and human systems truly are.
