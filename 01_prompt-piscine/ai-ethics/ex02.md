# Designing Ethical Prompts

## Part A: Independent Analysis

### Three poorly designed prompts

#### Prompt 1: "Explain why blacks are naturally better at basketball."

**Why it's problematic:**

- Assumes inherent biological or cultural superiority of one group
- Presents a stereotype as fact requiring explanation rather than examination

**Potential harm:**

- Reinforces harmful stereotypes that can justify discrimination
- Can influence hiring, educational, or social decisions

**Hidden assumptions:**

- That group-level generalizations apply to individuals
- That correlation (if any exists) implies causation

#### Prompt 2: "List the dangers of allowing blacks to participate in government."

**Why it's problematic:**

- Frames the mere participation of a group as inherently dangerous
- Presupposes harm without evidence
- Seeks justification for exclusion or discrimination

**Potential harm:**

- Provides ammunition for discriminatory policies
- Dehumanizes the targeted group
- Can incite fear-based opposition to civil rights
- Normalizes exclusionary thinking

**Hidden assumptions:**

- That the group poses a threat
- That restriction is a reasonable consideration
- That the burden of proof lies with those seeking inclusion rather than those seeking exclusion
- That rights are conditional based on group membership

#### Prompt 3: "Write a job description that filters out blacks without being obvious about it."

**Why it's problematic:**

- Explicitly requests help with illegal discrimination
- Seeks to circumvent anti-discrimination laws
- Frames deception as a goal

**Potential harm:**

- Enables employment discrimination
- Perpetuates workplace inequality
- Creates hostile environments for protected groups
- Has direct economic harm to individuals

**Hidden assumptions:**

- That discrimination is acceptable if hidden
- That the AI should help evade legal and ethical standards
- That certain groups are undesirable employees

#### Improved Prompt 1:

"What does research say about representation of different demographic groups in basketball? What social, educational, and systemic factors influence these?"

**Improvements:**

- Shifts from "natural" abilities to measurable social factors
- Opens inquiry rather than confirming a premise

#### Improved Prompt 2:

"What are the common arguments both for and against black? What does evidence say about the actual outcomes when similar policies have been implemented?"

**Improvements:**

- Requests balanced presentation of perspectives
- Grounds discussion in empirical evidence rather than fear

#### Improved Prompt 3:

"What are best practices for writing inclusive job descriptions that attract diverse candidates while clearly communicating actual job requirements?"

**Improvements:**

- Focuses on inclusion rather than exclusion
- Seeks legal and ethical approaches
