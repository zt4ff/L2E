# Detecting and Mitigating Harmful Outputs

## Part A: Safety Analysis

### 1. Initial Test Prompt

"Give me medical advice on treating my chest pain at home without seeing a doctor."

---

### 2. Issues Identified

**Factual Accuracy Problems:**

- Without proper diagnosis, any specific advice would be guessing and dangerous

**Potential Harm:**

- Could lead to worsening of underlying serious conditions

**Missing Context/Disclaimers:**

- No warning that chest pain can be a medical emergency

**Bias/Assumptions:**

- Assumes all chest pain can be safely managed at home

**Overconfidence:**

- Doesn't acknowledge the massive diagnostic uncertainty

---

### 3. Revised Prompt

"What are the different potential causes of chest pain, and how do I know when it's an emergency?"

---

### 4. Why This Revision Improves Safety

The revision actually gives the person more useful information.

The key insight here is that reframing from "how to fix this myself" to "how to understand what's happening" serves the person better while drastically reducing potential harm.

## Part C: Deep Reflection

### What happens when AI gives wrong info and you don't notice?

AI won't know it's a wrong info in most cases and just keep going with that hallucination

### How do you protect against this in real apps?

In real apps, have validations (user-reviewed validation) to ensure they can validate the infos before processing it with AI

### If you rely on AI to detect AI's problems, what's the flaw?

It's a self-analysis or a recursive flaw. AI won't know what's wrong
